# Title
Phase 4 Validation Decisions: P0 Acceptance Thresholds, QA Sampling Rules, and Launch Packet Structure

# What changed
- Defined Phase 4 validation hard gates and a weighted readiness score framework in the P0 acceptance matrix.
- Defined QA sampling operational rules for AI/HITL review (mandatory HITL + low-confidence + SLA-risk prioritization, provenance checks, reviewer outcomes).
- Standardized the launch/no-launch decision packet structure and decision checklist for founder sign-off.

# Why it changed
- Internal validation must produce a defensible launch/no-launch decision tied to measurable outcomes and safety/audit evidence, not subjective session impressions.

# Impact (UI / logic / data)
- UI: No UI change.
- Logic: Decision-making process is now standardized for P0 validation sessions.
- Data: Validation sessions are expected to generate structured scores, defect severities, and AI QA outcomes linked to evidence bundle files.
